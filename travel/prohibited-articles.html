Security

What not to pack on BC flights

LOS-ABV , PHC-LHR screening rules

NAHCO and international security standards apply. If an item is not listed here, call 070-4066-8343 before travel. Penalties include confiscation and denied boarding.

Restricted categories

Flammable items

  • Fuels, lighter fluid, and flammable paints
  • Fireworks and party poppers
  • Aerosols not for personal use (exceptions apply)

Toxic & corrosive

  • Bleach, drain cleaner, and acids
  • Pesticides and infectious substances
  • Radioactive materials

Weapons

  • Firearms and realistic replicas
  • Knives, scissors over 6 cm (carry-on)
  • Stun guns, martial arts weapons

Batteries

  • Spare lithium batteries over 160 Wh not permitted
  • Power banks only in carry-on (not checked)
  • Damaged or recalled batteries forbidden

Liquids (carry-on)

  • Containers max 100 ml in a 1-litre clear bag
  • Includes gels, pastes, and aerosols
  • Duty-free liquids in sealed STEB bags accepted

Medical & special items

  • Essential medicines with prescription or letter
  • Medical devices notify airline in advance
  • Sharp medical items declare at security

Quick FAQ

Can I carry palm oil or pepper soup in checked bags?

Properly sealed commercial food may be allowed domestically; liquids and strong-smelling items may be rejected. International sectors are stricter.

Power bank in hand luggage?

Yes under 160Wh, carry-on only. Not in checked baggage.

Carry-on vs checked

Item Carry-on Checked
Power bank Yes (limits apply) No
Sporting firearms No Yes (declared, unloaded)
Tools over 6 cm No Yes
Perfume (over 100 ml) No Yes

This page is a guide only. Final decisions rest with airport security and Blackcess ground staff. Regulations may differ on international sectors.
